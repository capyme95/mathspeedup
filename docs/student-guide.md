# MathSpeedup 2.0 – Student Guide

Welcome to **MathSpeedup**, a personalised learning dashboard designed to help you master NCEA Level 1 Mathematics (Standard AS91945) through evidence‑based learning techniques.

## Dashboard Overview

The dashboard is divided into four phases, each offering distinct tools to support your learning:

1.  **Evidence‑Based Learning** – learning intentions, worked examples, self‑reported grades.
2.  **Feedback & Mastery** – structured feedback and mastery charts.
3.  **Advanced Optimisation** – adaptive learning paths, retrieval practice, mastery forecasting, cognitive‑load optimisation, personalised feedback.
4.  **User Feedback & Monitoring** – a form to rate your experience and leave comments.

## How to Use Each Feature

### Learning Intention & Success Criteria
- **Purpose**: Clarifies what you’re about to learn and how you’ll know you’ve succeeded.
- **What to do**: Read the “Learning Intention” and “Success Criteria” at the top of the dashboard before starting a task. Use them as a checklist while you work.

### Worked Examples
- **Purpose**: Shows step‑by‑step solutions to typical problems, helping you build a mental model of how to approach similar tasks.
- **What to do**: Study the example carefully. Note how each step is justified. Try to explain the reasoning in your own words before moving on.

### Self‑Reported Grades
- **Purpose**: Encourages metacognition—thinking about your own learning. Research shows that students who predict their own performance learn more effectively.
- **What to do**: After completing a task, select the grade you believe you’ve earned (Achieved, Merit, Excellence). Be honest with yourself.

### Structured Feedback
- **Purpose**: Provides specific, actionable feedback on three levels: the **task**, the **process**, and your **self‑regulation**.
- **What to do**: Read each column of feedback. Use the “Next Steps” suggestions to guide your practice.

### Mastery Charts
- **Purpose**: Visualises your progress across the three TIMSS cognitive domains: **Knowing**, **Applying**, and **Reasoning**.
- **What to do**: Check the chart weekly. Aim for steady upward trends. If a domain plateaus, focus on that area in your next study session.

### Adaptive Learning Paths
- **Purpose**: Recommends the most beneficial next task based on your recent performance, cognitive load, and weakest TIMSS domain.
- **What to do**: Follow the recommendation when you’re unsure what to practice next. The system adapts as you improve.

### Retrieval Practice
- **Purpose**: Strengthens long‑term memory through active recall (the “testing effect”). Also helps you calibrate your confidence.
- **What to do**: Answer each mini‑quiz question, then rate how confident you were (1–5). Review the results to see where your confidence aligns (or doesn’t) with actual accuracy.

### Mastery Dashboard 2.0
- **Purpose**: Shows your mastery history with trend lines and forecasts, helping you see where you’re headed.
- **What to do**: Look at the forecast dots for each domain. If the trend is upward, you’re on track. If it’s flat or declining, consider adjusting your study habits.

### Cognitive‑Load Optimiser
- **Purpose**: Helps you monitor and reduce mental effort that doesn’t contribute to learning (extraneous cognitive load).
- **What to do**: Rate your cognitive load after each task (1–7). If it’s high, switch to the “Simplified View” to hide non‑essential interface elements.

### Personalised Feedback Engine
- **Purpose**: Generates custom feedback based on your performance patterns, cognitive load, and self‑reported grades.
- **What to do**: Read the feedback after each task. It will highlight strengths, pinpoint gaps, and suggest targeted practice.

### Feedback Form
- **Purpose**: Lets you tell us what’s working and what could be improved. Your ratings and comments are stored securely and reviewed regularly.
- **What to do**: Use the star rating (1–5) and comment box at the bottom of the dashboard whenever you have a suggestion or encounter a bug.

## Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **TIMSS** | Trends in International Mathematics and Science Study – a global assessment that classifies mathematical thinking into three cognitive domains. |
| **Cognitive Load** | The amount of working‑memory resources used during a task. High extraneous load can hinder learning. |
| **Self‑Reported Grade** | A prediction you make about your own performance before receiving external feedback. |
| **Prediction Accuracy** | How close your self‑reported grade was to the actual outcome (expressed as a decimal between 0 and 1). |
| **Logic Fingerprint** | A unique set of reasoning patterns captured from your task submissions, used to personalise feedback. |
| **Evidence‑Based Learning** | Teaching methods that have been validated by rigorous educational research (e.g., Visible Learning, Cognitive Load Theory, Testing Effect). |

## Tips for Success

1.  **Be consistent** – Visit the dashboard at least twice a week to maintain momentum.
2.  **Use the feedback** – Don’t just read it; act on the “Next Steps” suggestions.
3.  **Monitor your cognitive load** – If you feel overwhelmed, switch to the Simplified View and take a short break.
4.  **Embrace retrieval practice** – Even if you get answers wrong, the act of trying to recall strengthens memory.
5.  **Ask for help** – If something is unclear, use the feedback form to tell us. We’ll improve the dashboard based on your input.

## Need Help?

- For technical issues (e.g., the dashboard won’t load), contact your teacher or administrator.
- For questions about the learning content, refer to your NCEA Mathematics textbook or online resources.
- To suggest a new feature or report a bug, use the **Feedback Form** at the bottom of the dashboard.
