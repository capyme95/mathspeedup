# MathSpeedup 2.0 – Usability Test Script (Sebastian)

**Purpose**: Evaluate the dashboard’s clarity, usefulness, and ease of use through a 30‑minute guided session.

**Test Lead**: Teacher or parent (you).

**Participant**: Sebastian (student).

**Materials**: Computer with internet access, dashboard open at `https://mathspeedup.vercel.app`.

**Duration**: 30 minutes.

**Recording**: Optional screen recording (with participant consent) to capture interactions and facial expressions.

---

## Introduction (2 minutes)

1.  **Greeting & context**  
    “Hi Sebastian. Today we’re going to try out a new learning dashboard called MathSpeedup. It’s designed to help you get better at NCEA maths by giving you personalised feedback and practice recommendations. This is a prototype, so we’re very interested in your honest opinion about what works and what doesn’t.”

2.  **Consent**  
    “I’d like to record our session so we can remember your feedback better. Is that okay? The recording will only be used to improve the dashboard and will not be shared publicly.”

3.  **Instructions**  
    “I’ll ask you to complete a few tasks on the dashboard. There are no right or wrong answers—we’re testing the dashboard, not you. Please think out loud as you go, telling me what you’re looking at, what you’re trying to do, and any questions or confusion that comes up.”

---

## Task 1: Explore the Dashboard (5 minutes)

**Goal**: Assess first impressions and discoverability.

1.  “Please open the dashboard and look at the screen for a moment. Without clicking anything, tell me what you think this page is for.”
2.  “Now scroll down slowly. What sections do you notice? What catches your eye?”
3.  “Can you find the ‘Learning Intention’ and ‘Success Criteria’? Read them out loud.”
4.  “Find the ‘Worked Examples’. How many examples do you see? Do the steps make sense to you?”

**Observe**:

- Does the student immediately understand the dashboard’s purpose?
- Does the visual hierarchy guide their attention?
- Are terms like “TIMSS domain” or “cognitive load” confusing?

---

## Task 2: Submit a Self‑Reported Grade (5 minutes)

**Goal**: Test the self‑assessment workflow.

1.  “Imagine you’ve just completed a maths task on the topic ‘Linear Equations’. Look for the ‘Self‑Reported Grade’ component.”
2.  “Select a grade level—Achieved, Merit, or Excellence—that you think you would have earned.”
3.  “Now find the ‘Cognitive Load Optimiser’ nearby. Rate how mentally demanding you found the task (1 = very easy, 7 = very hard).”
4.  “Click ‘Submit Grade’. What happens?”

**Observe**:

- Can the student locate the grade selector and rating slider easily?
- Do they understand what “cognitive load” means in this context?
- Is the submission feedback clear?

---

## Task 3: Try Retrieval Practice (5 minutes)

**Goal**: Evaluate engagement with the testing‑effect module.

1.  “Scroll down to the ‘Retrieval Practice’ section. Read the explanation.”
2.  “Answer the three quiz questions. Before checking the answer, rate your confidence (1–5) for each.”
3.  “After seeing the correct answers, look at the ‘Confidence vs Accuracy’ chart. What does it tell you?”

**Observe**:

- Does the student engage with the confidence rating?
- Do they understand the connection between confidence and accuracy?
- Is the chart interpretation intuitive?

---

## Task 4: Interpret Personalised Feedback (5 minutes)

**Goal**: Assess the clarity and usefulness of generated feedback.

1.  “Find the ‘Personalised Feedback Engine’. Read the feedback shown.”
2.  “Can you identify which part of the feedback is about the task, which is about the process, and which is about self‑regulation?”
3.  “Look at the ‘Next Steps’ suggestions. Do they seem helpful? Would you know what to do next?”

**Observe**:

- Does the student distinguish between task/process/self‑regulation feedback?
- Are the “Next Steps” actionable?
- Does the feedback feel personalised or generic?

---

## Task 5: Adaptive Learning Paths & Mastery Dashboard (5 minutes)

**Goal**: Test comprehension of adaptive recommendations and mastery visualisations.

1.  “Look at the ‘Adaptive Learning Paths’ section. What does it recommend you should practice next? Why does it say that?”
2.  “Now examine the ‘Mastery Dashboard 2.0’ with the line charts. Which TIMSS domain are you strongest in? Which is weakest?”
3.  “What do the forecast dots (hollow circles) represent? What trend do you see for the ‘Reasoning’ domain?”

**Observe**:

- Can the student articulate the reasoning behind the recommendation?
- Do they understand the chart axes and legend?
- Can they make a simple prediction based on the trend line?

---

## Task 6: Provide Feedback via the Feedback Form (3 minutes)

**Goal**: Validate the in‑app feedback mechanism.

1.  “Scroll to the very bottom of the page. Find the ‘Phase 4 – User Feedback & Monitoring’ section.”
2.  “Rate your overall experience with the dashboard (1–5 stars).”
3.  “Write a short comment about one thing you liked and one thing you found confusing or would improve.”
4.  “Click ‘Submit Feedback’. What happens?”

**Observe**:

- Is the feedback form easy to find and use?
- Does the student feel comfortable submitting honest feedback?
- Is the submission confirmation clear?

---

## Debrief (5 minutes)

**Goal**: Collect overall impressions and suggestions.

1.  “Overall, how did you find the dashboard? Was it easy or difficult to use?”
2.  “What was the most helpful part? What was the least helpful?”
3.  “If you could change one thing about the dashboard, what would it be?”
4.  “Would you use this dashboard regularly to study maths? Why or why not?”
5.  “Is there anything else you’d like to tell us?”

**Thank the participant** and assure them that their feedback will be used to improve the dashboard.

---

## Post‑Test Notes

- **Technical issues**: Record any bugs, errors, or broken links.
- **Confusion points**: Note terminology or interactions that caused hesitation.
- **Delight moments**: Capture positive reactions or “aha” moments.
- **Suggested improvements**: List concrete changes the student proposed.

---

## Success Metrics

- **Completion rate**: Did the student complete all six tasks without major blockers?
- **Time on task**: Did each task take roughly the expected time?
- **Satisfaction score**: What star rating did they give in the feedback form?
- **Verbal feedback**: Were comments mostly positive, neutral, or negative?

---

*This script is a starting point. Adapt questions and tasks based on the student’s age, prior experience, and the dashboard’s current feature set.*
